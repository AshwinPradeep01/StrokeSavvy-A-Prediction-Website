StrokeSavvy

StrokeSavvy will help you to understand about Stroke (Cerebrovascular accident ) and how it occurs, what should you do when you encounter one. Everyone is vulnerable to stroke, although there are certain factors upon which the chances of stroke greatly depends like hypertension , gender, age, marriage, lifestyle, personal habits such as smoking and history of heart disease etc. Stroke happens when a clot or rupture interrupts blood flow to the brain. Ischemic caused by a clot, Hemorrhagic caused by a rupture and Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA) or “warning stroke” caused by a temporary blockage.

F.A.S.T. is an acronym used to teach the most common warning signs and sudden symptoms of stroke. F.A.S.T. stands for (F) face drooping, (A) arm weakness, (S) speech difficulty, (T) time to call 1-1-2(Emergency Helpline). Surviving a stroke increases the risk of a second one (1 in 4 survivors have another stroke). During a stroke, nearly 120 million brain cells die every hour which equals to aging 3.6 years approxiamately. Time lost is brain lost, so act quickly.
